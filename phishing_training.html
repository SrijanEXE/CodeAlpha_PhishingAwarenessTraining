üéØCommon Targets

Phishing attacks commonly target:

  • Credit card and payment information
  • Personal identification information
  • Corporate data and intellectual property
  • Access to internal systems and networks

üìßEmail Red Flags

Suspicious sender addresses that don't match the organization

Poor grammar, spelling mistakes, or awkward phrasing
Requests for sensitive information via email
Suspicious links or attachments

üé≠ Social Engineering Tactics

‚ö°Urgency and Fear

Attackers create artificial urgency to bypass your logical thinking:

Example: "Your account has been compromised! Click here within 24 hours to secure it or lose access permanently!"
Solution: Take time to verify. Contact the organization directly through official channels.

üéØAuthority and Trust

Phishers impersonate trusted figures or organizations:

  • Government agencies (IRS, FBI)
  • Banks and financial institutions
  • Popular online services (Google, Microsoft)
  • Company executives or IT departments

üéØSpear Phishing Case Study
